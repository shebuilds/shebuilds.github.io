SheBUILDS is a two-day, global hackathon hosted by Girl Who Code at Berkeley for high school and college female-identifying students. As a beginner-friendly hackathon, we aim to equip our participants with 21st-century skills in order to help close the gender gap in technology and to change the image of what a programmer looks like and does.

Ever since childhood, we've been asked "what do you want to do when you grow up?" The tech industry is intimidating, confusing, and even unreachable to many youths growing up.

SheBUILDS provides a unique opportunity for high schoolers and college students alike to innovate, connect, and collaborate with UC Berkeley women in STEM. Our hackathon exposes students to a variety of technologies and fields like Artificial Intelligence and web development and to inspire participants to fearlessly pursue a career in those fields. SheBUILDS encourages participants to build on newly learned technologies in order to make their highest contribution to society. We want our participants to help themselves to help others.

What is a hackathon, specifically SheBUILDS?

SheBUILDS is a beginner-friendly, two-day hackathon where participants will work collaboratively to propose and create innovative solutions to real-world issues with technology.

How do I apply for the hackathon?

  • Register as a student attendee here!
  • Register as a judge here!
  • Register as a mentor here!

I don't have any experience. Can I still participate?

Of course! We welcome all skill levels and backgrounds. Our workshops and guided tracks will set you up with everything you need to get started on your idea.

When are applications due?

Applications are due by Friday, April 30, 2021, at 11:59 PM PST (Pacific Standard Time).

When will application decisions be released?

Applications will be reviewed and accepted on a rolling basis.

Do I apply as a team or as an individual?

You will be applying as an individual. During the hackathon, you will have the opportunity to form groups of up to four students.

Who can participate in the hackathon?

High school and college non-binary/female-identifying students are welcomed to participate in the hackathon.

When is the hackathon?

The hackathon will be held virtually on May 1st from 9AM-8PM and May 2nd from 10AM-3PM PST (Pacific Standard Time). The entire hackathon will be two days long.

What platform will SheBUILDS be hosted on?

Our main source of communication will be Slack. You'll receive an invitation to join our Slack upon your acceptance. All events and workshops will be held on Zoom. The Zoom links will be detailed on our Slack as well.

What types of workshops are offered in the hackathon?

We offer TONS of beginner friendly workshops such as intro to hackathons, into to GIthub, intro to Artificial Intelligence, and much more!

Is there a code of conduct?

Yes, we abide by MLH's Code of Conduct.
